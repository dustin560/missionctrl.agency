The starting position

You said it clearly: Solmax is operating in the dark.

Decisions made on gut feel. Regions running their own P&Ls with no alignment. Five or more legacy businesses stitched together. A brand architecture that only went live at the end of 2024.

The new leadership team — CEO, COO, CFO — is 18 months in and positioning for growth. The CRM is being built. The brand tracker is on Giovanna's list. All the right bets are being made.

But there is a gap between the strategy and the stakeholder. And until you can measure it, you cannot close it.

That gap has a name: integrity. The alignment between what Solmax commits to (Intent), what stakeholders believe it stands for (Expectation), and what they actually experience (Reality). When those three drift apart, trust erodes quietly — and performance follows.

The good news: Solmax has not lost trust so much as it has not yet built it deliberately. That is a very different starting position. And it means the opportunity is real.

The difference between a company and a movement is integrity at scale: when what you believe, what you say, and what you do are the same thing — and stakeholders can feel it.

The ambition

Solmax as a movement, not just a manufacturer.

In our conversation, something important emerged. When we asked how Solmax should define itself — geosynthetics manufacturer, or protector of land and water through safety and circularity — Karen's answer pointed to something larger than a positioning statement.

Companies like Ørsted did not just change their own business. They rebuilt communities. They decarbonised a category. They created shared value at scale.

That is what it means to operationalise a purpose — not through campaigns, but through every interaction with every stakeholder, consistently over time.

Solmax has the same potential. The world's largest geosynthetics manufacturer, working at the intersection of sustainable construction, land protection, and water security.

If the Solmax promise is credible, consistent, and felt by the people who matter most — specifiers, contractors, regulators, communities, employees — it does not just win deals. It shapes how the category thinks.

The proposition

TrustOS becomes your brand tracker.

You are already planning to build a brand tracker. Giovanna has the brief. The CRM is being scoped. You have a live worldwide dashboard in mind by 2027. Here is how TrustOS plugs in — not as another tool, but as the operating system that connects them.

A conventional brand tracker measures funnel metrics: awareness, consideration, loyalty. TrustOS does something more powerful. It explains the why behind those numbers — which stakeholder relationships are strong, where belief is leaking, and what to do about it. Not instead of brand intelligence. A better version of it: one that gives leadership a clear signal, not more data to interpret.

Driver 01

Clarity

Telling a true story?

Whether stakeholders understand who Solmax is, what it stands for, and the role it plays in their world — across every region and every audience.

Driver 02

Connection

Do you deliver distinction?

Whether stakeholders feel something distinctive about Solmax — a relationship that goes beyond procurement, beyond the spec sheet.

Driver 03

Confidence

Does experience match expectation?

Whether what Solmax delivers — in product, in service, in conduct — actually matches what was promised, every time, in every market.

These three drivers sit on a foundation of Integrity — the overall alignment between what Solmax commits to, communicates, and delivers. The stronger the integrity, the higher the trust. The higher the trust, the stronger the brand.

The approach

A phased rollout — fast to approve, fast to start, immediately useful.

We recommend starting simple and building over time. Two steps to baseline. A quarterly rhythm to sustain.

Step 01

Set-up & Onboarding

Before we collect a single data point, we align on what the dashboard needs to show. This is a structured session with Karen and Giovanna to agree:

  • Stakeholder groups and segmentation (by region, vertical, relationship type).
  • The metrics that matter for each stakeholder group.
  • Data sources available now vs. gaps that need research.
  • How TrustOS fits alongside — or becomes — the brand tracker.
DimensionData Required
Intent Corporate strategy, leadership priorities, strategic bets, stakeholder value framework, ESG and governance commitments.
Expectation Brand positioning and messaging, communications archive (press, case studies, sustainability content, social), tone and voice guidelines.
Reality Stakeholder survey outputs (Trust Index, promise and message perception, open text), employee engagement data, retention/NPS/CSAT where available. Where gaps exist, we design and deploy targeted surveys.

Where data gaps exist, we design and deploy targeted surveys — with a standard template you can reuse each quarter, either managed by us or run in-house.

Step 02

Snapshot & Solutions

Once the baseline is live, TrustOS runs on a quarterly cadence:

  • Updated stakeholder data and trust scores.
  • A "what changed" narrative tied to events, initiatives, and delivery shifts.
  • Updated Clarity, Connection and Confidence driver breakdown.
  • Insights and recommended actions for the next quarter.

This is not a one-off audit. It is an operating rhythm — the ongoing intelligence that keeps Giovanna's dashboard live and gives leadership a reliable signal year on year.

Phase A

Sense

Baseline trust across stakeholders and identify the biggest leaks in belief. Better signal, better feedback.

Phase B

Solve

Select 1–3 solutions with clear owners, actions, and measurable outcomes — focused where impact is highest.

Phase C

Sustain

Track movement, publish proof internally (and externally where appropriate), repeat quarterly.

Optional — Year Two+

Stakeholder panels & synthetic focus groups.

If Solmax wants a more sustainable, lower-effort insight engine over time, we can build two complementary capabilities into TrustOS.

Always-on signal

Quarterly stakeholder panels

Standing groups of specifiers, owners, contractors, regulators (plus employees where relevant) that provide stable trend data, qualitative depth on why scores move, and early warnings before they hit revenue.

Decision support

Synthetic audience tool

Built from population or panel inputs to simulate stakeholder responses to comms and proof-point testing, service or experience changes, innovation and product concepts, and major strategic trade-offs — before you commit.

Together, panel data feeds the synthetic audience tool — an always-on focus group integrated into TrustOS. Test the message before you spend the budget. Test the change before you ship it.
